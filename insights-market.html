Market Intelligence

Mastering Market Seasonality

Real estate is cyclical. We track inventory volume and days-on-market trends to deploy your budget exactly when seller motivation is peaking.

Most investors deploy their marketing budget in a flat line, spending the same amount in December as they do in May. This is a mistake. The market breathes in predictable cycles, and by analyzing Inventory Volume and Days on Market (DOM), we can predict exactly when homeowners are most likely to negotiate.

1. The Inventory Cycle

According to national housing data, "Active Listings" fluctuate wildly depending on the season. In the spring, inventory spikes as families try to move before the school year. For wholesalers, this means more competition. We pivot your strategy during these high-volume months to focus on speed-to-lead, ensuring you touch new listings before the bidding wars begin.

2. Days on Market (DOM) is Leverage

The "Days on Market" metric is the single best indicator of seller motivation. When this number trends upward (typically in late fall and winter), buyers retreat, and sellers get nervous. This is the Wholesaler's Goldmine. We increase dial volume specifically targeting stale listings during these windows, knowing that an offer that was rejected in June might be accepted in November.

3. Timing Your "Ask"

We don't just call random numbers; we forecast. By overlaying "Pending Sales" data against "New Listings", we identify markets where supply is outpacing demand. In these pockets, we train our VAs to be more aggressive with price negotiation, because the data proves the seller has fewer options.
